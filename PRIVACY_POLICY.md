# Privacy Policy for Scarlet>fire

**Last updated:** January 14, 2026

## Overview

Scarlet>fire is a music streaming application that provides access to Grateful Dead concert recordings from the Internet Archive. This privacy policy explains how the app handles information.

## Data Collection

We are committed to protecting your privacy. Scarlet>fire:

- **Does not collect** any personal information
- **Does not require** account registration or sign-up
- **Does not use** analytics, tracking, or advertising services
- **Does not share** any data with third parties

## Local Data Storage

- **Favorite Shows**: Your favorite shows list is stored locally on your device only and is never transmitted to our servers or any third party.
- **App Preferences**: Any app settings or preferences are stored locally on your device.

This data remains on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

## Third-Party Services

### Internet Archive

Scarlet>fire streams audio content directly from archive.org (Internet Archive). When you stream a concert:

- Your device connects directly to Internet Archive's servers
- Internet Archive may collect standard web server logs (IP address, etc.) according to their privacy policy
- We recommend reviewing the [Internet Archive Privacy Policy](https://archive.org/about/terms.php) for more information

### No Other Third-Party Services

The app does not integrate with any other third-party services, social media platforms, or advertising networks.

## Permissions

The app requests the following permissions:

- **Internet Access**: Required to stream audio from the Internet Archive
- **Audio Playback**: Required to play music in the background

## Children's Privacy

Scarlet>fire does not knowingly collect any information from children. The app is designed to stream publicly available concert recordings and does not require any personal information from users of any age.

## Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted in the app and on this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

## Content Rights

All Grateful Dead concert recordings available through this app are sourced from the Internet Archive and are made available with the permission of the Grateful Dead and their representatives. The Grateful Dead has granted permission for fans to record and trade live performances for non-commercial purposes.

## Your Rights

As we do not collect any personal information, there is no personal data to access, modify, or delete. However, you have complete control over:

## Data Security

Since we don't collect or transmit personal data, there is no risk of your personal information being compromised through our app. All streaming connections to the Internet Archive use industry-standard HTTPS encryption.

## California Privacy Rights
